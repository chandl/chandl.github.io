About Me

Ever since I was little, software development has been my passion. I still remember writing my first programs -- I was trying to make game mods in an obscure programming language, with no idea of what I was doing... Throughout middle school and high school, I attempted to build my knowledge and satiate my curiosity by learning about game development. I watched way too many YouTube videos and arguably spent way too much time at my computer; however, I was able to go from "Hello World" to building Mario-like games, with dozens of projects in-between! I learned a C-like language, then JavaScript, Java, and experimented with a bunch of others in between.

Since then, this passion has carried me through my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science and is driving me now as a part-time online graduate student at Georgia Tech and in my full-time contract role as a Software Engineer at Nike. I've been so grateful to be able to study and learn more about this field, all with pretty much the same fascination that drove me as a kid.

So, now in the industry, this passion remains with me. I most certainly have more to learn, more problems to solve, and more projects to be a part of. For this, I am very grateful to my younger self, and I am looking forward to what's to come.
